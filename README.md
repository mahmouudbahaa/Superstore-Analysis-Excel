The Superstore business is growing steadily in terms of sales, but profitability is not following the same trend. At first glance, the company looks successful — however, a deeper look into the data shows that profit is being affected by pricing and discount decisions rather than demand.

This analysis was done to understand whether growth is actually efficient or just volume-driven.

-Objective: Understand the relationship between sales and profit Measure the impact of discounting on profitability Identify where profit is being generated or lost Evaluate performance across regions, products, and pricing levels

-Dataset: Source: Superstore Dataset (Kaggle) Includes: Sales, Profit, Discount, Region, Category, Sub-category, Segment

-Data Preparation: Cleaned missing and inconsistent values Standardized data formats Structured data for analysis using pivot tables Created price range segments for deeper comparison

-Key Findings: Sales vs Profit Total Sales: 2.29M Total Profit: 286K ** Sales are high, but profit represents only a small fraction of revenue, showing efficiency gaps in conversion from sales to profit.

-Regional Performance: West region contributes ~32% of total profit (strongest performer) Central region contributes only ~14% of profit (weakest performance) ** Significant imbalance in profitability across regions.

-Discount Impact (Statistical Insight): A clear pattern emerged from regression analysis:

Discounts above ~20% are strongly associated with profit decline High-discount transactions often show reduced or negative profit ** This indicates a negative relationship between discount and profitability.

-Product & Pricing Insights: ~72% of total profit comes from mid-range products (100–1000) Low-price products (<100) contribute negative or very low profit A small number of sub-categories generate most of the profit

-Key Insight: The business is not struggling with sales — it is struggling with profit efficiency.

Growth exists, but it is being reduced by aggressive discounting and uneven product performance.

-Recommendations: Reduce unnecessary discounts, especially above 20% Focus on high-margin product categories Re-evaluate low-performing regions (especially Central) Shift focus from sales volume → profit optimization

-Tools Used: Excel | Pivot Tables | Regression Analysis | Descriptive Statistics | Data Visualization

-Conclusion: This project shows that revenue growth alone is not enough to judge business performance. Understanding statistical relationships between discount, sales, and profit is key to improving overall business efficiency.
